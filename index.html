No sale on record at all, and last received more than the selected window ago. Quantities are rounded down to full pack sizes (conversionFactor) before valuing — loose, partial-pack units are excluded from the value.

Has a sale history, but nothing sold in the selected window, and last received more than that same window ago. Mutually exclusive with Never Sold — this tab only covers items that sold at some point. Quantities down-rounded to full pack sizes before valuing.

The source data's "netQty100Days" field is actually a 90-day net sold quantity. Target 100d Stock = (Sold /90d ÷ 90) × 100 — the amount needed to cover 100 days at the current sell-through rate. Excess = current stock beyond that target, valued at unit price, no pack rounding. Items with zero sales in the last 90 days are excluded here — they show up under Never Sold or Dead Stock instead.

Stock is greater than zero but the pack size (conversionFactor) is missing, zero, or invalid — down-rounding to a pack isn't reliable, so these are excluded from Never Sold and Dead Stock and just listed here as raw data for review. (They can still appear under 100-Day Excess, since that calc doesn't depend on pack size.)

Stock is currently zero, so there's nothing to value — listed here as raw reference data only, excluded from all three main reports.
